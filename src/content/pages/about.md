# Who we are

The CUNY AI Lab (CAIL) is a faculty- and staff-led initiative for experimentation with large language models and other approaches to machine learning by CUNY researchers across disciplines. Based at the CUNY Graduate Center, the Lab supports work with AI that prioritizes ethical practice and aligns with CUNY's public mission.

# What we do

CAIL responds to the rapid adoption of AI in higher education by building infrastructure designed around real and identified campus needs while remaining responsive to a rapidly transforming, often in-flux landscape. The Lab's work is organized around experimentation and aims to produce purpose-driven projects developed in response to concrete research and teaching questions. Our goal is to deconstruct the discourse around generative AI that frames it as a single, abstract technology, in favor of building a grounded form of literacy that emphasizes user agency and treats AI as a set of techniques that can be prototyped, evaluated, and revised, allowing faculty, staff, and students exercise control and choice with the tools they use.

# Where we draw from

CAIL draws on recent work such as the Google.org-sponsored [Critical AI Literacy Institute (CALI)](https://criticalai.commons.gc.cuny.edu/), as well as initiatives like the [CUNY Academic Commons](https://commons.gc.cuny.edu/) and [Manifold Scholarship](https://cuny.manifoldapp.org/), which reflect the Graduate Center's longstanding commitment to creating accessible, scalable digital infrastructure for and with CUNY faculty, students, and staff. Building on these efforts, we are piloting CAIL in a select number of courses across CUNY while working toward broader access over time. We are also piloting a series of workshops focused on AI fundamentals. As we scale, we are committed to developing infrastructure grounded in privacy, sustainability, transparency, and critical thinking—and to providing CUNY faculty, staff, and students with reliable access to AI tools that make data retention policies, model selection, and evaluation practices explicit.
